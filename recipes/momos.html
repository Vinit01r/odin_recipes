Momos

Description

Momos are a type of steamed dumpling that originated in Tibet and Nepal but have become a beloved street food across India and other parts of Asia. Made from a simple dough of refined flour, these bite-sized delights are filled with flavorful mixtures such as finely chopped vegetables, chicken, paneer, or meat. The filling is wrapped in thin dough sheets and then steamed until soft and tender. Momos are usually served hot with a spicy red chutney or mayonnaise, creating a perfect balance between the mild, juicy dumplings and the fiery dip.

Beyond just being a snack, momos represent comfort and community. They’re often enjoyed with friends at roadside stalls or cafes, especially during evenings. Over time, many variations have emerged — like fried momos, tandoori momos, and even cheese-filled or chocolate momos — each with its own twist on the original recipe. Their soft texture, flavorful fillings, and spicy accompaniments have made momos one of the most loved fast foods in India and beyond.

Ingredients

  • For the Dough

  • 1 cup refiend flour
  • 1/4 tbsp salt
  • 1/2 tbsp oil
  • Water

  • For the Vegetable Filling

  • 1 cuo finely chopped cabbage
  • 1/2 cup grated carrot
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped capsicum
  • 1 tbsp finely chooped garlic
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped ginger
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1/2 tbsp black pepper powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp oil

  • For the Chutney

  • 3-4 dried red chilies
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 2-3 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 tbsp vinegar or lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dough

    1. In a mixing bowl, add 1 cup refined flour (maida) and ¼ teaspoon salt.
    2. Add a few drops of oil and mix well.
    3. Slowly add water and knead into a smooth, soft dough (like chapati dough).
    4. Cover it with a damp cloth and rest for 15–20 minutes.

  2. Make the Vegetable Filling

    1. Heat 1 teaspoon oil in a pan.
    2. Add garlic and ginger, sauté for a few seconds.
    3. Add onion, carrot, cabbage, and capsicum. Stir-fry on high flame for 2–3 minutes.
    4. Add salt, pepper, and soy sauce. Mix well.
    5. Turn off the flame and let the mixture cool completely before filling.

  3. Shape the Momos

    1. Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls.
    2. Roll each ball into a thin, small circle (about 3–4 inches wide).
    3. Place 1 spoonful of the filling in the center.
    4. Fold the edges together — you can make them half-moon shaped or pleated round momos.
    5. Seal tightly so the filling doesn’t spill out while steaming.

  4. Steam the Momos

    1. Grease a steamer plate (or use an idli stand if you don’t have one).
    2. Arrange the momos so they don’t touch each other.
    3. Steam for 10–12 minutes until the outer layer becomes slightly shiny and firm.
    4. Remove carefully — they’re now ready to serve!

  5. Make the Chutney

    1. Boil or roast 2 tomatoes and 3–4 dried red chilies.
    2. Blend them with 2–3 garlic cloves, salt, and a little vinegar or lemon juice.
    3. You’ll get a spicy, tangy chutney perfect for dipping.

  6. Serve and Enjoy

    • Serve hot momos with spicy chutney or mayonnaise.
    • Enjoy your soft, juicy homemade momos!
