Burger

Description

A burger is a popular fast food item made by placing a cooked patty—usually made of minced meat like beef, chicken, or vegetables—between two halves of a soft, round bun. It is often layered with fresh ingredients such as lettuce, tomato slices, onions, and cheese, and topped with condiments like ketchup, mayonnaise, or mustard. The combination of a juicy patty, crispy vegetables, and flavorful sauces makes the burger both satisfying and versatile, allowing it to be enjoyed in countless variations around the world.

Beyond just being a quick meal, the burger has become a global culinary icon. From street food stalls to gourmet restaurants, it’s served in many forms — from the classic cheeseburger to modern plant-based versions. Burgers represent comfort, convenience, and creativity in food culture. Whether grilled at a family barbecue, ordered at a fast-food joint, or crafted with gourmet ingredients, a burger always delivers a delicious mix of textures and flavors that make it universally loved.

Ingredients

  • Burger Buns

  • 2 burger buns
  • 1 tbsp butter

  • Patty

  • 2 boiled potatoes
  • 1/4 cup boiled peas
  • 1/4 cup grated carrots
  • 2 tbsp bread crumbs
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • Salt(to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala or mixed herbs
  • Oil(for shallow frying)

  • Toppings

  • 2 cheese slices
  • 2 lettuce leaves
  • 1 tomato
  • 1 small onion
  • 2-3 pickle slices or jalapenos

  • Sauces & seasoning

  • 1 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 1 tbsp tomato ketchup
  • 1 tsp mustard sauce
  • A pinch of black pepper or chili flakes

Steps

  1. Boil and Mash the Vegetables

    • Boil 2 potatoes, ¼ cup peas, and ¼ cup carrots (and other veggies like beans or corn if you like).
    • Once soft, mash them well in a large bowl until smooth.

  2. Season and Mix

    • Add the following to the mashed vegetables:
      • Salt (to taste)
      • ½ tsp red chili powder
      • ½ tsp garam masala or mixed herbs
      • 2 tbsp bread crumbs
      • 1 tbsp cornflour or maida (for binding)
    • Mix everything well until it forms a dough-like mixture that can hold its shape.

  3. Shape and Cook the Patty

    • Take a handful of the mixture and shape it into a round, flat patty (like a cutlet).
    • Heat some oil in a pan and shallow-fry the patty on medium heat until it turns golden brown and crispy on both sides.
    • Place them on tissue paper to absorb extra oil.

  4. Toast the Burger Buns

    • Slice the burger buns in half.
    • Spread a little butter on the inner sides and toast them lightly on a pan until golden.

  5. Assemble the Burger

    1. Place the bottom half of the bun on a plate.
    2. Add a lettuce leaf.
    3. Place the fried veg patty on top.
    4. Add a cheese slice (optional).
    5. Place tomato and onion slices.
    6. Spread mayonnaise and ketchup (or any sauces you like).
    7. Cover with the top bun.

  6. Serve and Enjoy

    • Secure the burger with a toothpick if needed.
    • Serve hot with fries, ketchup, or cold drink on the side!
